# ReviewRush Timing Guide
## When to Ask for Reviews (And When NOT To)

---

## üéØ THE PERFECT TIMING FORMULA

**Ask within the "Golden Window":**

```
Service Completed ‚Üí 30 minutes to 24 hours ‚Üí ASK
```

After 24 hours, response rates drop by 50%.
After 7 days, most people forget the experience entirely.

---

## ‚è∞ TIMING BY BUSINESS TYPE

### Restaurants & Cafes
- **Best time:** While they're finishing their meal or at checkout
- **Second best:** Within 2 hours via text
- **Method:** Table tent with QR code + follow-up SMS

### Home Services (Plumber, Electrician, HVAC)
- **Best time:** Right after completing the job, in person
- **Second best:** Same-day email (within 4 hours)
- **Method:** Verbal ask + leave-behind card

### Retail Stores
- **Best time:** At checkout after positive interaction
- **Second best:** Next-day email if you have their info
- **Method:** Receipt QR code + verbal ask

### Professional Services (Lawyers, Accountants, Consultants)
- **Best time:** After delivering positive outcome
- **Second best:** 24-48 hours after final meeting
- **Method:** Personal email from the professional

### Healthcare & Wellness
- **Best time:** At checkout, after appointment
- **Second best:** Same-day email
- **Method:** Front desk verbal ask + email follow-up

### Auto Services
- **Best time:** When handing over keys
- **Second best:** Same-day text
- **Method:** Verbal ask + SMS link

## üìä RESPONSE RATES BY TIMING

| When You Ask | Expected Response Rate |
|--------------|------------------------|
| In person, same moment | 40-60% |
| Within 1 hour (text/email) | 25-35% |
| Same day | 15-25% |
| Next day | 10-15% |
| 2-3 days later | 5-10% |
| 1 week+ later | <5% |

**Lesson:** Ask IMMEDIATELY or within hours, not days.

## üö´ WHEN NOT TO ASK

### Never Ask:
- ‚ùå Right after a complaint (even if resolved well)
- ‚ùå When customer is visibly stressed or rushed
- ‚ùå During a problem or issue
- ‚ùå If they haven't received the full service yet
- ‚ùå Multiple times in the same visit
- ‚ùå To the same customer within 90 days

### Wait and Ask Later:
- üü° Customer was neutral (wait for follow-up positive interaction)
- üü° Service was delayed or had hiccups (wait 24 hours, check in first)
- üü° Complex service not yet complete (wait until fully delivered)

---

## üìÖ THE 7-DAY FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE

**Day 0:** Service completed
**Day 0-1:** First ask (in-person or immediate email/text)
**Day 3:** Follow-up if no response
**Day 7:** Final ask (then stop)

After 3 attempts with no response, move on. Don't harass.

---

## üéØ THE ASK FORMULA

1. **Confirm satisfaction first**
   - "How was everything today?"
   - "Did we take care of everything?"

2. **Wait for positive response**
   - If negative: Address the issue, DON'T ask for review

3. **Make the ask**
   - "Would you mind sharing that on Google?"

4. **Make it easy**
   - Send link immediately or hand them QR code

5. **Thank them regardless**
   - "Thanks either way!"

---

## üí° PRO TIPS

### Tip 1: Create a Trigger
Identify the moment in your service when customers are happiest. That's your trigger to ask.

### Tip 2: Automate the Follow-Up
Send automated email/SMS but make it feel personal. Use their name and reference the specific service.

### Tip 3: Track Your Asks
Know who you've asked so you don't repeat. Simple spreadsheet works.

### Tip 4: Train Your Team
Everyone should ask. Create a contest - who gets the most reviews this month?

### Tip 5: Respond to Every Review
When you respond to reviews (especially negative ones professionally), future customers see you care. This encourages more reviews.

**Your competitors aren't asking.** This is your advantage.
